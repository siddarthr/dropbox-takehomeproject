Data Analysis - US Government jobs


Introduction:

The US government has a number of job openings posted on its webiste. This is an effort to analyse the jobs available based on departments, geographies, roles and how long jobs have been open for. The dataset for this project was taken from USA JOBS.

Analysis:

From the Geo chart below, we can see that the states with most number of US govt job openings are Maryland and Virginia. A hypothesis for this could be the fact that many agencies and departments are center around the captial of the country - DC



If we categorize the jobs based on how long they have been open for, we can see that almost 75% have been open for a long time (i.e, more than 60 days). The two 'New' categories combine to form only 20% of the total jobs.

Categorizing the same by departments, we see that most of the old jobs stem from the Department of Homeland Security. One possible reason could be that the agency employs extensive recruiting procedures to make sure their employees can work with very sensitive data. Another story the chart tells is that the proportion of "old jobs" is high in Department of Labor as well.



The following two charts categorizes the number of jobs in each department based on type of role (i.e. Full time, Part Time or Other). Department of Agriculture has the most number of jobs and almost 90% of them are Full time roles. And from the second chart, we can see that the only departments with a significant number of part time roles are Dept. of Homeland Security and Other Agencies and Independent Organizations. It is also interesting to note that a good proportion of listing in Dept. of Health and Human Services are classified as "Other" as the work type in the listing didn't have words that were highly indicative of them falling in the other two categories.
